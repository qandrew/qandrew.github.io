First Week in Beijing

Hello World, China

September 13, 2015

Beijing, China: Here is my obligatory Hello World post about my time in Beijing. I arrived a week ago, and I spent the past few days setting up my life in this familiar yet unaccustomed country. I am spending this fall studying abroad at Peking University (北京大学), and I am excited to take a couple of math and history classes ...taught in Chinese. We'll see how it goes.
中国北京：这是我的第一个关于我的北京生活的博客发布。我上周到达北京，这前几天就在适应我又熟悉又不了解的北京与中国，今年秋天我在北大留学，明天课程开始，我期待着 我选的数学和历史课。。。但是课是用中文教的。看着办吧，希望不太难。

What have I been up to? - 这几天我干了什么？

These past few days have been quite chill. There aren't many orientation events here at PKU (unlike at MIT), so I've had some time to set up my life here (find housing, get a cell phone, etc) and relax. I have met exchange students from Sweden, Japan, Korea, Russia, Germany, France, America, and many other places. I haven't seen much of Beijing so far, but I hope to explore the city and also take trips around China. Though I've visited Beijing multiple times as a tourist, this is first time that I am living in Beijing; I'll see things from a different and fresh perspective. Last Sunday, we had an orientation event to the Mutianyu Great Wall.
这几天挺放松的。北大没有很多新学生活动（不像MIT），所以我有时间办事（找房子，买电话），也有时间放松。我认识了一些留学生，有从瑞典， 日本，韩国，俄罗斯，德国，法国，美国，和其他国家来的。我还没去转北京，但希望能在中国和北京旅游。虽然我以前作为游客来过北京， 这学期是我第一次在北京生活，我会对北京有新的观点。上周七我们北大留学生去了慕田峪长城。

Great Wall. Contrary to Western media, blue skies aren't too uncommon in Beijing

Today my roommates and I tried to visit Mao's Maosoleum, but when we got to Tiananmen Square, the line to enter the Maosoleum was over two hours long. It's not the best idea to visit tourist attractions during the weekend.
今天本来想跟室友去看毛主席纪念堂，可是到了天安门广场，看到得排两个小时的队。还是工作日去参观跟好。

Tiananmen Square. Commemorating the 70th anniversary of the end of World War II

Where do I want to head? - 我的目标是什么?

I have three main goals for my time here at Peking University.
今年秋天在北大有三个主要目标。

Firstly, I want to immerse myself in Chinese culture and try to fully blend in. I hope to improve my Chinese to a point such that others won't be able to tell I grew up outside of the country.
一，我想潜入中国文化，融入中国生活。我想提高中文，到别人不能认出我是国外长大的。

Secondly, I want to meet other Chinese people around my age who grew up in China, and make strong friendships that will last. Though I have relatives in China, I do not know many people my age.
二，我想认识跟我年龄相似的国内长大的中国人，建立深厚的友谊，将能维持下来。虽然在中国有亲戚，我不认识很多同年龄人。

Finally, with time away from the Institute, I also want to spend this fall thinking about how I want to spend my last two years in college. I want to spend time thinking about what I truly care about, what I truly like, instead of simply spending time at MIT conforming and following trends.
三，在我离开麻省时，我也想好好想大学最后两年该怎么过。我想弄清楚我真的对什么感兴趣，喜欢什么，而不是只在MIT合乎大众或跟着别人走。

I hope to write a blog post once every two weeks, to keep y'all updated with my experiences in the motherland.
我争取每两周写博客，让你们知道我在中国的经历。

An orientation session at Beida

Setting up my life in Beijing - 北京头周生活

Entering a new country means that a lot of the things one takes for granted has to be set up again. Similar to my experience in Germany this summer, many of the things I take for granted in the US I have had to set up here once again in China.
去了新国家就得重新办很多事情：买手机，找住宿，等。跟今年夏天在德国的经验相似，在美国以想当然的事在中国又得重新办。

  • Cell Phone I got a student plan from China Mobile for 200RMB for ten months (there is no half year plan). It's a pretty good deal, considering monthly plans start from 60RMB. This gives me enough data and minutes, but the plan itself is quite complicated: every month I have 200mb of data usable throughout the country, 800mb only inside Beijing, 1GB during evening periods (11pm - 7am), and an additional 1GB summer and winter vacation. Wut duh?
    手机：我买了中国移动新生校园卡，用十月200元（没有半年套餐）。价钱挺好，一般的手机套餐一月60元起。我有足够流量和通话时间，可是套餐 挺复杂：每月有200mb国内流量，800mb北京市内流量，1gb闲时流量（23：00-7：00），寒暑假再加1gb。有意思。

  • Housing I applied for housing too late so I couldn't get a room at the Global Village or Shaoyuan, the two dorms for exchange students close to campus. I entered Beijing without a permanent place to stay. Fortunately I found housing at another PKU Dorm, Wanliu. I am now sharing a six-person, three-bedroom suite, complete with a kitchen and living room; its quite comfortable. My roommates are Russian, Korean, Japanese, and Spanish.
    住宿：因为登记晚了，没有拿到离校园进的中关新园或勺园宿舍里的房间。一般留学生会住那里。我到北京时，住宿还没定。 到了之后幸好找到了北大万柳宿舍。我现在住三居室，这套房也有厨房和大厅，还挺舒服。我的室友是俄罗斯，韩国，日本，西班牙人。

  • Bike Because transportation in Beijing during rush hour is simply awful, a five kilometer bus ride from my dorm to campus can become an hour long ordeal. Thus I bought a bike, and hopefully my commute will only be 20 minutes. People here are, well, a lot more aggressive than in the US. I'll have to pay full attention while on the road otherwise I may get hit...
    自行车：因为北京交通上下班时实在太挤，从万柳去北大可能会花一小时。因此我买了自行车，希望只用二十分钟骑车上学。这边人比美国人开车更猛， 骑车时必须很注意，要不然可能被撞。。。

  • Classes Exchange Students by default take Chinese language classes at PKU, and during orientation there is a placement test to determine your Chinese proficiency. (See this link for a description of the test.) Somehow I managed to pass the test and place out of Chinese language classes. I'll be taking normal college classes with the local college students. Oh boy. Classes start tomorrow (9/14); I haven't finalized my selection, but I hope to take a couple of math, philosophy, and history classes.
    选课：一般留学生在北大只上汉语课，在新生报道是有一个中文测验，为了测试中文水平来分班。 不知如何，我考过了测验，所以不用上汉语课。我会选本科生的课。 明天（九月十四）北大开学，我还没定下我所选的课，打算选一些数学，哲学，和历史课。

  • Other Things Bank Accounts, Internet VPN, medical insurance, etc.
    其它事：银行账户，上网VPN，医疗保险，等。
