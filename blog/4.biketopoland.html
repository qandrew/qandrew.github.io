I biked to Poland

100km Through Cities, Forests, and Farmlands

August 8, 2015

Berlin, Germany: I had two weekends in Germany. Having explored quite a bit of Berlin, I became more interested in exploring the areas around Berlin. I knew I wanted to enter Poland before I left Europe, since the closest point, Slubice (which is across the Oder river from the German city of Frankfurt an der Oder), lay only 100km away.

But which Polish city should I see? Warsaw is 6 hours away by train, and 2 hours by plane, at this point unaffordable by my summer intern budget. Poznan is 3 hours away, which I heard is a relative large and interesting Polish city. I could have also gone to Szczecin, a port city about 2 hours away by train. (Szczecin is along the route of the Berlin-Königsberg highway, one of Hitler's projects from Nazi Germany. Some parts of this highway today are still unmodified and unrepaired from that era.)

Then a crazy idea entered my mind: I would bike to Poland, since I wanted to explore the surroundings of Berlin by bike, and I also wanted to go to Poland. Since Frankfurt and Slubice are only 100km away, I could make that trip in half a day! I looked on Google Maps, and found a route that could take me there:

I began the bike trip at 8am in the morning. Though having slept for only 5 hours (bad life decisions last night), I was energetic and ready to go! My mindset for the trip was bike-first, photo-second, since I wanted the trip to focus more on the biking. It was a pretty journey through many terrains: on ashphalt through the city, dirt and sand (which sucks) through the woods, farmlands, and finally Poland. This was definitely one of the memorable experiences of my summer in Berlin.

Six hours, 5 liters of water, one massive Döner, and tons of sweat later, I made it to Poland! Frankfurt an der Oder and Slubice are two small towns, definitely not touristy, so there wasn't much to see. Having entered Poland, I bought some Polish Beer, got some Polish currency, and took some photos, before taking the RE1 back to Berlin (no, I wasn't going to bike back). What did I get out of the trip? Having the whole day to myself, disconnected from the internet, I had some time to think about what I like to do, and who I really am. The short answer: I don't know. Having exited high school and lost my two primary interests (Swimming and League of Legends), I've been drifting a bit in college. It has been a great two years so far, but I still don't know what sport I really like, what profession I should go into, how to be a good friend, along with many other life questions.

Maybe I want to go on more biking trips? I don't know. Maybe I should figure out what I want to do with my life? Easier said than done. Someone told me recently, it's a good idea to find an interest that also allows you to meet new people. But I guess with time, I should find some answers.

Note: I didn't want to make this blog post too long, but feel free to contact me if you want to hear more about the journey! I'd also love to go biking with you sometime :)
