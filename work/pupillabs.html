Pupil Labs

Working at a startup in Berlin, Germany

Posted August 27th, 2015: After my sophomore year, I spent the summer of 2015 working at Pupil Labs in Berlin, Germany. I found the internship through the MIT MISTI-Germany program, with Erin helping me tremendously throughout the process (thanks so much!). Pupil Labs is a startup that designs and sells eye-tracking headsets, and this summer I worked on improving the eye-tracking algorithms.

Summary of the Project

I worked with my boss, Moritz, to develop a 3D eye model algorithm proposed by a Cambridge University research paper. The key idea behind the algorithm is that with the video stream, the eye pupil will be recorded from multiple angles. The eye pupil will appear as an ellipse (since it is a projected circle) on the video screen, and thus it is possible to reconstruct the 3D eyeball by unprojecting the ellipses into back into 3D space (as circles) and constructing an eyeball sphere that best fits the circles. This model will better pupil detection in edge cases; for example when a complete pupil ellipse cannot be seen.
