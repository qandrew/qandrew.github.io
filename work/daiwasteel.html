Daiwa Steel

Speaking Japanese, Seeing Steel

Posted January 24th, 2016: During the IAP (MIT's January break) of my Junior year, I ventured to Japan where I externed at Daiwa Steel. The company's headquarters and factory are located in Tochigi Prefecture in Japan, approximately an hour north from Tokyo. The company was founded in 1932 and specializes in creating steel tubes, with applications in construction scaffolding and greenhouse framework. Currently the company is led by Mr. Shinichiro Nakamura, a graduate from the MIT Sloan School of Management.

Summary of the Project

I spent time analyzing the company's current meeting software, and provided recommendations for how to use technology to improve workplace productivity. We were also given a tour of Daiwa's steel factory, and subsequently devised recommendations to improve workplace safety.

Life in Utsunomiya and Japan

We lived in the company dorm, about a ten minute drive from the factory. I had my personal room, and the dorm shared a Japanese ofuro (bath) and lounge, which we would use to relax after a day of work. The closest sizable city from our factory is Utsunomiya, a city famous for its dumplings (gyoza). We took trips to the gyoza restaurants. From Utsunomiya to Tokyo we used the Shinkansen to travel in an efficient manner. Being in quite a local Japanese setting, we also were able to experience a lot of authentic Japanese culture. Every morning we greeted our fellow employees by saying お早うございます (good morning), and bowing was a common custom to show respect.
