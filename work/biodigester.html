Biodigester

Your waste isn't that wasteful

Posted June 13th, 2015: For the Spring 2015 Semester, I took the D-Lab:Energy (ec.711) class at MIT. I worked with Janet Lin, Nani Ruiz, and Elaine Kung to develop a biodigester for families in rural El Salvador. We visited El Salvador over Spring Break and designed surveys to determine the feasability of biodigesters there.

Summary of the Project

What is Biodigestion? In a sealed environment, bacteria in manure will conduct anaerobic respiration, which produces methane usable for cooking. A biodigester is a sealed environment where manure and water is inserted, and methane, fertilizer is the output. This technology both manages waste and provides cooking energy.

What did the project entail? After visiting El Salvador, we realized that the current existing biodigester design at D-Lab was not suitable for our target families in El Salvador. Thus, we designed our own biodigester, based on a trash can or 55-gallon drum, and built a prototype with manure inserted (ewww!) on the D-Lab roof.
