Testing Parallax

Mar 8, 2019 2 min read blog articles

Andre showed us around the SpaceX headquarters on Thursday night, and it really was an eye-opening watershed moment for me. I had always thought of Space Exploration as something that “the other people” did, in that we would hear about NASA launches but it always felt far away. This time around Andre took us to the factory, showing posters of the Falcon 9, the Falcon Heavy, the Dragon, and the BFR / Starship that would be massive in size, literally of science fiction proportions, yet realistically launching within a few years. This week especially had a lot of hype, as Friday night saw the successful launch of the first crewed dragon pod, and in the wee hours of Sunday morning the dragon pod successfully docked to the ISS. We saw the construction of the actual Dragon 2 pod, which later this year would be the first time that astronauts would travel on commercial spacecraft to the ISS, and the first time since the end of the Space Shuttle in 2011 that Americans would be launched to space by American technology from American soil (currently they must use Russian Soyuz spacecraft which are launched from Russian-leased land in Kazakhstan). Seeing in person was such a wowing moment for me, as I could actually work at SpaceX and work on bringing humanity to mars. The epiphany that with computer science skills I had the actual opportunity to work on these science-fiction like endeavors was very exciting and inspiring.
